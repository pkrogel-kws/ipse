NA15CENOV SES3-311 11/11/2015 8:30:00 AM 11/11/2015 10:00:00 AM Predicting a Product Quality Attribute by Incorporating Raw Material Parameters Using Multivariate Modeling: A Case Study A particular impurity is a critical product quality attribute (PQA) in one of our commercial biotechnology processes and is known to be highly dependent on the variability of a chromatographic purification resin used. A collaboration with the resin vendor is ongoing, aimed at understanding the source of the variability and eventually control it. In support of this collaboration, a Multivariate Data Analysis (MVDA) model was built that takes into account both the characteristics of the resin and the process material, as well as key process operating parameters. Using a training set from commercial and small-scale model (SSM) data, the MVDA model predicts the impurity levels to within the same accuracy of the laboratory SSM. The MVDA model identified which resin Certificate of Analysis parameters contribute most significantly to the impurity levels, which now allows the resin vendor to focus their investigation into those sources of variability. Problem or Opportunity Statement:

The assessment of critical quality attributes, and the control of critical process parameters that impact these attributes, is one important component of the overall control strategy for drug substance and drug product manufacturing. For process parameters, there are many different approaches for assessing criticality, and statistics can play an important role in these evaluations, when data is available to support the assessment.

Learning Objectives:

  • The described statistical methodology is intended to provide a consistent framework for team discussions on the criticality of process parameters
